Audio Quality Evaluation

Task overview

You will listen to short speech recordings and rate them on three dimensions: the speech signal, the background noise, and the overall quality. The recordings come from real-time MRI speech, so the voices and background may sound unusual — that is expected.

Important notes

  • Please use headphones in a quiet room.
  • Some samples with clear, expected answers are included as quality checks. If you fail more than half of these, your work may be rejected.
  • The task takes approximately 20–25 minutes.

Instructions

For every sample you will rate the same three things, always in this order:

1. The SPEECH SIGNAL. Attend only to the speech (the voice itself), ignoring any background noise. How distorted does the speech sound?
2. The BACKGROUND. Attend only to the background noise, ignoring the speech. How intrusive or noticeable is the background?
3. The OVERALL quality. Now consider the sample as a whole. What is its overall quality?

You may replay each clip as many times as you like, but you cannot return to a sample once you click Next. A short practice session comes first.
